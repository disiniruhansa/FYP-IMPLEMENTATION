PERIOD CRAMPS AND PAIN

What it is:
Period cramps happen when the uterus tightens to release menstrual blood. Many girls feel cramps in the lower belly or back.

What is normal:
Mild to moderate cramps are common, especially on the first one or two days of a period.

What may help:
Using a warm water bottle or heating pad, resting, gentle stretching, light walking, and drinking water can help reduce discomfort.

What is not normal:
Pain that is so strong that you cannot go to school, fainting, vomiting, or pain that does not improve at all.

When to seek help:
If cramps are very severe, last many days, or stop you from doing daily activities, it is important to talk to a doctor or clinic.
