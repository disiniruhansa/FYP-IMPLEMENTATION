LATE OR MISSED PERIODS (TEENAGERS)

What it means:
A period can sometimes come earlier or later than expected. In teenagers, periods are often irregular during the first 1–3 years after the first period.

Common reasons:
Stress, school exams, lack of sleep, sudden weight changes, illness, heavy exercise, and normal hormone changes can delay a period.

What is usually normal:
Being late by a few days or even a couple of weeks can happen sometimes. Occasional irregular periods are common in teenagers.

What you can do:
Try to rest, eat regular meals, drink enough water, and manage stress. Keeping a simple period calendar can help you understand your cycle.

When to seek help:
If your period has not come for 2–3 months, if you have strong pain, dizziness, fainting, or very heavy bleeding, talk to a doctor or clinic.
If you are sexually active and miss a period, pregnancy is possible and medical advice is important.
