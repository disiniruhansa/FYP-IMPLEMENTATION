LATE OR IRREGULAR PERIODS (COMMON CAUSES)

Why it happens: Stress, exams, changes in sleep, illness, travel, sudden weight changes, heavy sports, and normal hormone shifts can all make a period come later than expected. In the first 1–3 years after your first period, it is common to be irregular.

What to watch: Track your cycle dates, sleep, and stress. If you have very heavy bleeding, severe pain, fainting, fever, or feel unsafe, reach out to a trusted adult or a clinic.
