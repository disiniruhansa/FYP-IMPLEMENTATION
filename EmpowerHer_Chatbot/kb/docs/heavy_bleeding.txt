HEAVY PERIOD BLEEDING

What it means:
Some girls have heavier periods than others. Heavy bleeding means soaking through pads very quickly or bleeding for many days.

Signs of heavy bleeding:
Needing to change pads every 1–2 hours, passing large blood clots, feeling very tired, dizzy, or weak.

What you can do:
Rest, drink water, and eat balanced meals. Keep track of how many pads you use per day.

When to seek help:
If you feel dizzy, faint, short of breath, or soak pads very fast, you should talk to a doctor urgently.
Heavy bleeding can cause low iron levels and should not be ignored.
